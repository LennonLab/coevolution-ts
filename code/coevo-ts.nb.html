Stability

From QB:

The stability of an aggregate measure of an ecological system can be assessed by measuring its variability. One way to characterize variability is the Coefficient of Variation (CV). The CV relativizes the standard deviation of a variable to its mean value because variance scales with the mean. By using the CV, we can more easily compare the variability of systems with different mean values. We can calculate the CV as follows: \[CV = \frac{\sigma}{\mu}\] where \(\sigma\) is the standard deviation and \(\mu\) is the mean value. Higher CV indicates more variability, and lower CV indicates less variability. Therefore, we can measure stability as the inverse of CV: \[\textrm{Stability} = \frac{1}{CV}\]
